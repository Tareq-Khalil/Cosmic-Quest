Welcome to COSMIC QUEST

COSMIC QUEST is an international astronomy competition organized by the STEM October Astronomy Club (SOAC) specifically designed for grade 9 and high school students worldwide.

Our Mission

The purpose of COSMIC QUEST is to attract attention to the wonders of the cosmos through an engaging two-round challenge that tests your knowledge and passion for astronomy and astrophysics.

Who Can Participate

Eligibility: Open to all high school students and grade 9 students

Team Size: Each team must consist of exactly three members

Global Competition: Students from around the world are welcome

Core Competition Topics

Five Essential Areas of Astronomical Knowledge

1. Introduction to Astronomy

Fundamental principles of astronomical observation, celestial mechanics, and the structure of the universe.

2. Stellar Astronomy

Star formation, stellar evolution, stellar classification, and the life cycles of stars from birth to death.

3. Planetary Science

Solar system dynamics, planetary formation, exoplanets, and comparative planetology.

Core Topics Continued

4. Galactic & Extragalactic Astronomy

Structure and dynamics of galaxies, galaxy classification, dark matter, and large-scale structure of the universe.

5. Cosmology & Astrophysics

Big Bang theory, cosmic microwave background, dark energy, and the evolution and fate of the universe.

Master these concepts to excel in COSMIC QUEST!

Competition Structure

Two Exciting Rounds Await

How to Participate

Teams must register online before the deadline. Once registered, you'll receive:

  • Access to the competition platform
  • Detailed instructions via email
  • Practice materials and resources
  • Technical support contact information

Ready to Begin?

Register your team and start your cosmic journey!

First Round: Open Round

Format & Details

Questions: 30 analytical questions covering all key astronomy and astrophysics topics

Duration: Three days (September 17-20, 2025)

Flexibility: Teams can participate at their own pace during the competition window

Tracking: Live scoreboard displays real-time team rankings

What to Expect

The first round will test your foundational knowledge across all five core topics. Questions will range from basic concepts to more complex analytical problems that require critical thinking and application of astronomical principles.

Second Round: Invitational Round

Selection Criteria

Top-ranked teams from the first round will be invited based on their final scores and rankings. Only the best performers advance to this elite round!

Live Monitoring Requirements

Screen Sharing: Participants must share their screen throughout the round

Camera On: Keep your camera on to ensure integrity and fairness

Supervised Environment: Professional proctoring ensures fair competition

Final Challenge Format

30 Advanced Multiple Choice Questions

60 Minutes Time Limit

Simultaneous Competition for All Teams

September 30, 2025 at 3:00 PM GMT

Team Formation & Rules

Essential Guidelines for Participation

Team Requirements

Team Size: Exactly three students per team

Grade Level: Currently enrolled in grade 9 or high school (grades 9-12)

Registration: All team members must register together under one team name

Limitation: Each student can only be part of one team

Code of Conduct

Academic Honesty: Teams are expected to maintain the highest standards of academic integrity

Respectful Behavior: Professional and respectful conduct is required throughout the competition

Zero Tolerance: Any form of cheating, plagiarism, or inappropriate conduct may result in immediate disqualification

Communication Rules

Round-Specific Guidelines

First Round: Team members may collaborate freely with each other during the 3-day window

Second Round: External assistance or unauthorized resources are strictly prohibited during the 60-minute live session

Fair Play: Only collaboration between registered team members is allowed

Academic Integrity

We trust all participants to maintain the highest standards of academic honesty. Any violation of these rules will result in immediate disqualification and may affect future participation eligibility.

Support Available

Technical support and clarification on rules will be available throughout both rounds of the competition.
